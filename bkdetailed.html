Booking.com

Due to confidentiality, I cannot disclose all the details for these projects and many more that I have worked on. So here are some highlights of the work I did and have been doing for the past 4 years at Booking.com:

Deals and Marketing campaigns

  • Full ownership of UX design for worldwide marketing campaigns (Black Friday, Cyber Monday, Easter, New Year, and many others);
  • Full-funnel UX+UI Design for every platform (Web, Mobile web, IOS and Android apps), landing pages design and HTML+CSS coding;
  • Refactoring of tools used by partners to join a campaign and set up deals;
  • Collaboration with different areas of the company for the global release of the campaigns;
  • In collaboration with copywriters, product managers and researchers, ran dozens of sessions of user research and hundreds of A/B testing experiments for campaigns optimization;
  • Millions of users impacted.

How did we get there?

The main problems and challenges we were trying to solve were:

  • Make it easy for a partner to join a campaign;
  • Upgrade partner-facing product, which was poorly optimized and not scalable to our new needs;
  • Communicate the discounts and the promotions for the users in all platforms, also using different marketing channels;
  • Increase conversion and bookings.
Step 1: Redesigning the campaigns hub

Why?

In order to create a deal, the partner would have to go through eight steps. Select type of deal, select target, length of stay, discount amount…

This caused friction and confusion for them, mostly because they didn’t know how do an optimal setup of a deal. That would cause a lot of trouble if we wanted to introduce a new type of deal/campaign that they would have to setup all by themselves.


How?

Removing steps and filling pre-selected options made the partners create the discount and join the campaign in just a few clicks.

Step 2:Improving customer experience with campaigns

Why?

Customers coming from every channel of traffic would land on the index page and didn’t know where to search for the Deals.

So a landing page would become the bridge between the marketing campaigns and the hotels offering those deals.


How?

Deals page designed and coded by me after collecting feedback from user interviews, testing prototypes and constantly optimized from experimentation.

Third-party inventory

  • Responsible for driving a complete redesign and defining a new value proposition for one of the main Pricing products. Worked closely with copywriters and Product Managers to define a new vision and strategy for the future.
  • Ran countless rounds of user research and user testing with different prototypes. Dozens of UX improvements and iterations.
  • Impact: Comparing the versions in one year period - huge decrease in customer support inbound, big increase in clarity as shown in user feedbacks and surveys, increase in conversion.
